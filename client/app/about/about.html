What is MTC?

The Metropolitan Transportation Commission (MTC) connects Bay Area communities by allocating regional, state and federal funds for transportation projects, by planning for the future, and by coordinating the participation of governments and residents in the planning process. MTC is the transportation planning, financing and coordinating agency for the San Francisco Bay Areaâ€™s nine counties: Alameda, Contra Costa, Marin, Napa, San Francisco, San Mateo, Santa Clara, Solano and Sonoma. MTC regularly updates the Regional Transportation Plan, a comprehensive blueprint for the development of mass transit, highway, airport, seaport, railroad, bicycle and pedestrian facilities. MTC also functions as the Bay Area Toll Authority (BATA) and the Service Authority for Freeways and Expressways (SAFE) and manages a variety of operational programs such as FasTrak, Clipper and the 511 Traveler Information System.

About this Website

The purpose of this website is threefold:

  • To provide a means for project sponsors to add, review and edit projects they wish to have considered for the update to the current Regional Transportation Plan (RTP), Plan Bay Area.

  • To allow MTC to provide transparency throughout the Call for Projects process, and adoption of the new RTP.

  • To provide the general public with the means to view and query projects that are approved and included in the updated RTP.
